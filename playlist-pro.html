Discovery: Behavioral Patterns Drive Churn

Analysis of 125K customers across 19 variables revealed no single factor explained churn‚Äîinstead, behavioral patterns signaled disengagement.

Behavioral factors cluster together, driving churn patterns

Key Churn Indicators:

  • Low Engagement: <10 weekly listening hours = dramatically higher churn
  • Service Friction: 74% of high-inquiry customers churned vs. minimal churn for low-inquiry users
  • Content Mismatch: High skip rates signal recommendation-preference misalignment
  • Subscription Pauses: 3+ pauses = elevated risk
  • Age U-Curve: Under 25 and over 60 churn more than middle-aged users

Predictive Modeling: Identifying At-Risk Customers

Built and compared 4 ML models (Logistic Regression, Stepwise Logistic, Random Forest, XGBoost).

XGBoost won with 84.79% accuracy and 0.94 AUC‚Äîreliably ranking customers by churn risk.

Top Churn Predictors (Cross-Model Validation)

  1. Subscription Type: Free/student tiers churn at far higher rates
  2. Weekly Listening Hours: Strongest behavioral churn signal
  3. Customer Service Inquiries: High frequency = unresolved friction
